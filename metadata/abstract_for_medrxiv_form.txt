Background: Threshold-based cardiometabolic labels are clinically useful but do not fully represent continuous multivariable biomarker structure. Unsupervised phenotyping can describe such structure, but biomedical clusters require leakage control, stability assessment, robustness testing, and replication before interpretation. Objective: To identify and stress-test label-free cardiometabolic biomarker phenotypes in US adults using public NHANES data. Methods: Discovery used 6,048 adults from NHANES August 2021-August 2023; temporal replication used NHANES 2017-March 2020. Objective examination and laboratory biomarkers spanning age, adiposity, blood pressure, glycemia, lipids, renal function, inflammation, and hematologic domains were represented using TruncatedSVD and UMAP and clustered using HDBSCAN. Disease labels, self-reported diagnoses, demographic and socioeconomic variables, and survey weights were excluded from primary clustering. Results: The final solution identified three non-noise biomarker-density phenotypes plus an algorithmic noise/outlier group. Mean pairwise adjusted Rand index was 0.9935, mean pairwise normalized mutual information was 0.9885, mean non-noise silhouette was 0.9168, and mean noise rate was 7.10%. Lipid feature-block removal was the most destabilizing ablation, and best temporal-replication profile correlations ranged from 0.714 to 0.909. Conclusions: A leakage-controlled SVD-UMAP-HDBSCAN workflow identified stable exploratory cardiometabolic biomarker-density tendencies in NHANES adults. Findings are descriptive and exploratory, not diagnostic, causal, treatment-directing, or a clinical decision tool.
